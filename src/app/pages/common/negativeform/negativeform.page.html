Negative Form
In Finnish, the negative form is a distinguished verb form. Unlike in many languages, the negative word (verb) conjugates and not the verb in itself which will keep a stable form according to the tense. In present, you can form it by taking the first person of the positive form, for instance tulen (I come), and removing the n. Our negative stem would then be : tule.
This rule applies for any verb. Following is an example on how you would conjugate tulla
